5 Signs Your Furnace Needs Service Before Edmonton's Winter

Edmonton winters are no place to gamble on a failing furnace. Watch for rising heating bills, uneven warmth between rooms, strange rumbling or squealing sounds, frequent cycling, and a pilot light that burns yellow instead of blue. Catching these early lets JUJCO's technicians fix the issue before the first deep freeze, often for a fraction of an emergency-repair cost.

How to Lower Your Summer Cooling Bills

A few simple habits and a spring tune-up can trim 15 to 20 percent off your cooling costs. Replace filters monthly, keep vents clear, set the thermostat a degree or two higher, and have JUJCO clean the coils and check refrigerant levels. A properly charged, debris-free system runs shorter, cooler cycles and lasts years longer.

Why a Spring HVAC Tune-Up Pays Off

After a long Edmonton winter, your system deserves a check-up before summer demand arrives. A tune-up includes a full safety inspection, burner and heat-exchanger check, airflow measurement, and a condensate-drain clearing. Customers routinely report steadier temperatures, fewer breakdowns, and lower energy use through the year.

What to Do When Your AC Stops Cooling

Before you call, check the thermostat setting, the breaker, and whether the outdoor unit is blocked by leaves or snow. If the air is warm or the unit won't start, shut it down and call JUJCO, because running a failing compressor can turn a small fix into a full replacement. Our 24/7 line means help is never far away, even during a July heat wave.
